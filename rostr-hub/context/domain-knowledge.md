## Exercise Science Fundamentals

### Progressive Overload

**Principle**: Muscles adapt to stress by growing stronger. To continue progress, gradually increase:
- Weight/resistance
- Reps per set
- Sets per workout
- Frequency per week
- Range of motion
- Time under tension

**Application**: Plans should increase difficulty every 2-4 weeks based on user performance.

### Training Splits

**Full Body** (3x/week):
- Best for: Beginners, time-constrained users, fat loss
- Structure: Hit all major muscle groups each session
- Recovery: 48 hours between sessions

**Upper/Lower** (4x/week):
- Best for: Intermediate, balanced development
- Structure: Upper body 2x/week, lower body 2x/week
- Recovery: 72 hours per muscle group

**Push/Pull/Legs** (6x/week):
- Best for: Advanced, muscle building
- Structure: Push (chest/shoulders/triceps), Pull (back/biceps), Legs
- Recovery: Each muscle group trained 2x/week

**Bodyweight Circuit** (3-5x/week):
- Best for: Beginners, no equipment, fat loss
- Structure: Full-body circuits with minimal rest
- Recovery: Can be done more frequently due to lower intensity

### Rep Ranges & Goals

| Goal | Reps | Sets | Rest | Intensity |
|------|------|------|------|-----------|
| Strength | 3-6 | 3-5 | 2-3 min | 85-95% 1RM |
| Hypertrophy | 8-12 | 3-4 | 60-90 sec | 70-85% 1RM |
| Endurance | 15-20+ | 2-3 | 30-45 sec | 50-70% 1RM |
| Power | 1-5 | 3-5 | 2-3 min | Explosive |

**Lola's Default**: Hypertrophy range (8-12 reps) for most users, as it builds muscle and strength.

## Biomechanics & Form

### Compound vs Isolation Exercises

**Compound** (multi-joint):
- Examples: Squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows, pull-ups
- Benefits: More muscle activation, functional strength, time-efficient
- **Lola Priority**: Build programs around 3-5 compound movements

**Isolation** (single-joint):
- Examples: Bicep curls, tricep extensions, leg extensions
- Benefits: Target specific muscles, address weaknesses
- **Lola Priority**: Add 1-2 isolation exercises as accessories

### Movement Patterns

Every program should include these patterns:

1. **Squat Pattern** (quads, glutes): Squats, lunges, step-ups
2. **Hinge Pattern** (posterior chain): Deadlifts, RDLs, hip thrusts
3. **Push Pattern** (chest, shoulders, triceps): Push-ups, bench press, overhead press
4. **Pull Pattern** (back, biceps): Rows, pull-ups, face pulls
5. **Carry Pattern** (core, stability): Farmer's walks, suitcase carries
6. **Anti-Rotation** (core): Planks, dead bugs, Pallof press

### Form Cues Library

**Squat**:
- Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
- Push knees out over toes
- Chest up, neutral spine
- Sit back into hips
- Drive through heels

**Deadlift**:
- Hinge at hips, not lower back
- Bar close to shins
- Neutral spine (no rounding)
- Engage lats (pull bar into body)
- Drive hips forward to stand

**Push-Up**:
- Hands shoulder-width
- Body in straight line (plank position)
- Lower chest to ground
- Elbows at 45-degree angle
- Full range of motion

**Row**:
- Flat back, slight hip hinge
- Pull elbow toward ceiling
- Squeeze shoulder blade
- Control the eccentric
- Don't use momentum

## Nutrition Fundamentals

### Macronutrient Basics

**Protein**: 
- Role: Muscle repair, recovery, satiety
- Target: 0.8-1g per pound of body weight
- Sources: Chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, legumes, Greek yogurt

**Carbohydrates**:
- Role: Energy for workouts, glycogen replenishment
- Target: 40-50% of calories (adjust based on activity)
- Sources: Rice, oats, potatoes, fruits, whole grains

**Fats**:
- Role: Hormone production, nutrient absorption, satiety
- Target: 20-30% of calories
- Sources: Avocado, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish

### Calorie Targets by Goal

**Fat Loss**: 
- Deficit of 300-500 calories below maintenance
- High protein to preserve muscle
- Adequate fats for hormones

**Muscle Gain**:
- Surplus of 200-300 calories above maintenance
- High protein for muscle synthesis
- Sufficient carbs for training energy

**Maintenance**:
- Eat at maintenance (TDEE)
- Balance macros
- Focus on nutrient density

**Lola's Approach**: Provide general guidance, not specific meal plans (unless user provides dietary restrictions).

## Injury Prevention

### Common Issues & Prevention

**Lower Back Pain**:
- Cause: Poor hip hinge form, weak core
- Prevention: Master hip hinge, strengthen core, deadlift with proper form
- Cue: "Keep spine neutral, hinge at hips"

**Shoulder Pain**:
- Cause: Overhead pressing with poor mobility, internal rotation
- Prevention: Include face pulls, external rotation work, proper warmup
- Cue: "Pack shoulders down and back"

**Knee Pain**:
- Cause: Knees caving in during squats, weak glutes
- Prevention: Strengthen glutes, cue knees out, proper foot position
- Cue: "Push knees out over toes"

### Recovery Protocols

**Active Recovery**:
- Light cardio (walking, cycling)
- Mobility work
- Stretching
- Frequency: On rest days

**Sleep**:
- Target: 7-9 hours/night
- Muscle repair happens during deep sleep

**Hydration**:
- Target: Half body weight (lbs) in ounces of water
- More during intense workouts

**Rest Days**:
- Minimum: 1-2 per week
- Muscles grow during rest, not during workouts

## Periodization

### Linear Periodization

**Week 1-2**: Adaptation (lighter weight, higher reps)  
**Week 3-4**: Hypertrophy (moderate weight, 8-12 reps)  
**Week 5-6**: Strength (heavier weight, 5-8 reps)  
**Week 7**: Deload (reduce volume by 40%)  
**Repeat**

### Undulating Periodization

**Monday**: Hypertrophy (8-12 reps)  
**Wednesday**: Strength (4-6 reps)  
**Friday**: Power/Endurance (15-20 reps or explosive)

**Lola's Default**: Linear periodization for beginners, undulating for intermediate+

## Motivation & Psychology

### Habit Formation

**Strategies**:
- Start small (3 workouts/week, not 7)
- Consistency > intensity
- Track progress visually (streak counters, checkmarks)
- Celebrate small wins
- Remove friction (lay out workout clothes the night before)

### Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Motivation

**Extrinsic**: External rewards (weight loss, aesthetics, compliments)
- Short-term effective, long-term limited

**Intrinsic**: Internal rewards (feeling strong, stress relief, energy)
- Long-term sustainable

**Lola's Approach**: Celebrate extrinsic wins, but cultivate intrinsic motivation through education and empowerment.

### Overcoming Plateaus

**Signs of Plateau**:
- No strength gains for 3+ weeks
- No body composition changes
- Workouts feel too easy

**Solutions**:
- Increase volume (add 1 set)
- Increase intensity (add weight)
- Change exercises (new stimulus)
- Take a deload week (allow recovery)
- Reassess nutrition (are calories aligned with goal?)

## Safety Guidelines

### Red Flags - Stop and Seek Medical Help

- Sharp, shooting pain
- Joint pain (not muscle soreness)
- Numbness or tingling
- Pain that worsens with movement
- Pain that persists beyond 3 days

### Green Lights - Normal

- Muscle soreness 24-48 hours post-workout (DOMS)
- Feeling tired during workouts
- Temporary muscle fatigue
- "Burn" sensation during exercise

### Modification Principles

**Too Hard**:
- Reduce weight/resistance
- Decrease reps
- Simplify movement (e.g., push-ups on knees)
- Add more rest

**Too Easy**:
- Increase weight/resistance
- Increase reps
- Add complexity (e.g., single-leg variations)
- Reduce rest time
- Increase tempo control
